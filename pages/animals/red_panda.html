Scientific Name

Ailurus fulgens

Physiological Info

Red pandas are about the size of a large house cat, weighing 7-14 pounds (3-6 kg) and measuring 20-26 inches (50-65 cm) in body length, plus a 12-20 inch (30-50 cm) bushy tail. They have thick reddish-brown fur, a long ringed tail, white facial markings, and semi-retractable claws for climbing. Their ankles are extremely flexible, allowing them to climb down trees headfirst. They have a low metabolic rate similar to sloths, which helps them conserve energy on their low-nutrition bamboo diet.

Natural Habitat

Red pandas live in temperate forests in the Himalayas and mountainous regions of Nepal, India, Bhutan, China, and Myanmar. They prefer high-altitude forests (6,000-12,000 feet / 1,800-3,700 meters) with dense bamboo understory, moderate temperatures, and plenty of tree cover. They're arboreal creatures that spend most of their time in trees.

Diet

Despite being classified as carnivores, red pandas are primarily herbivorous. Their diet consists of about 95% bamboo (leaves and shoots), supplemented occasionally with fruits, berries, acorns, mushrooms, birds' eggs, insects, and small mammals. They have to eat 20-30% of their body weight daily (about 2-4 pounds of bamboo) because bamboo is so low in nutrients and they can only digest about 24% of what they consume.

Fun Facts

  • They have a "false thumb" (extended wrist bone) that helps them grip bamboo, evolved independently from giant pandas.
  • Red pandas are crepuscular (most active at dawn and dusk) and spend up to 13 hours a day foraging for food.
  • They're excellent climbers but awkward on the ground, often standing on their hind legs when threatened to appear larger.
  • Listed as Endangered with fewer than 10,000 individuals left in the wild due to habitat loss and poaching.
  • They communicate through various sounds including squeals, twitters, and huff-quacks.
