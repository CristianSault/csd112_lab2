Ailurus fulgens Red pandas weight 7-14 pounds (3-6 kg) and measure 20-26 inches (50-65 cm) in body length, plus a 12-20 inch (30-50 cm) bushy tail. Red pandas live in temperate forests in the Himalayas and mountainous regions of Nepal, India, Bhutan, China, and Myanmar. Red pandas are primarily herbivorous.
Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris Capybaras weight 77-146 pounds (35-66 kg) and measure about 3.5-4.4 feet (106-134 cm) in length and 20-24 inches (50-62 cm) tall at the shoulder. Capybaras inhabit densely vegetated areas near bodies of water across South America, including countries like Brazil, Venezuela, Colombia, Argentina, and Paraguay. Capybaras are strict herbivores that primarily graze on grasses and aquatic plants
Ambystoma mexicanum Axolotls are aquatic salamanders that typically measure 6-18 inches (15-45 cm) in length and weigh 2-8 ounces (60-225 grams). Axolotls are endemic to Lake Xochimilco and Lake Chalco in the Valley of Mexico, near Mexico City. Axolotls are carnivorous predators that primarily feed on small aquatic prey.
Octopus vulgaris They range greatly in size depending on species—the common octopus averages 12-36 inches (30-90 cm) and weighs 6. Octopuses are found in oceans worldwide, from shallow tide pools to depths exceeding 13,000 feet (4,000 meters), though most species prefer coastal waters. Octopuses are carnivorous hunters that primarily feed on crustaceans (crabs, shrimp, lobsters), mollusks (clams, snails), and fish.
